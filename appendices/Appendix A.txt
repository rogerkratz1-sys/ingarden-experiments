Appendix A ó Robustness to Event Segmentation

Overview
This appendix evaluates how motif assignments change when the Ten Virgins parable is eventized three ways: canonical (12 events), Parse A (13 events), and Parse B (11 events). It summarizes the rationale, the featurelevel behavior that drives motif stability, and the mapping and agreement procedures used to compare parses. Full reproducible artifacts, perpermutation tables, and adjudication candidates are archived in Supplement S1 (commit 7350a47; see outputs/motif_stability_per_permutation.csv and the mapping scripts).

A.1 Motivation
Segmentation alters the poset and therefore the space of admissible linear extensions. We treat the canonical eventization as the discovery regime and ask whether motif identities defined there remain meaningful when the same pipeline and thresholds are applied to nearby alternative parses. This isolates segmentation effects from retuning or rediscovery.

A.2 Cohesion Features
Connective and cohesion proxies (POSweighted connectives, connective density, PCA composite) are largely invariant to small segmentation changes because lexical content is fixed and connectives map consistently to sentence units. These features therefore provide a stable, contentanchored baseline for comparing structural diagnostics across parses.

A.3 Displacement Features
Positionbased features (disp_kappa, disp_excess, span metrics) shift predictably with event granularity: splitting an event increases (n) and can inflate perevent spans or normalized displacement. These shifts are systematic and accounted for in the mapping procedure, so they rarely change motif identity when the structural signature (block moves, anchor preservation, longrange move) remains intact.

A.4 Motif Stability (summary evidence)
Applying the canonical motif rules to Parse A and Parse B and mapping parse clusters back to canonical motif names by majority vote yields modest clusterñlabel agreement (Adjusted Rand Index ? 0.193 for Parse A; 0.186 for Parse B) and permotif preservation patterns reported in Supplement S1. APD is highly preserved (?0.96ñ0.99), DCO and BRE show partial preservation, while FM/EM, parts of GD, and some Other exemplars are most sensitive to splitting/merging. Individual permutations and borderline cases are exported for adjudication in S1.

A.5 Segmentation Vectors and Posets
The appendix includes the three segmentation vectors and the explicit posets for each parse so readers can inspect how adjacency covers change. These textual vectors are provided verbatim and are the inputs used by the deterministic mapping and stability scripts documented in S1.

A.6 Summary and reproducibility pointer
Motif identities are structurally robust to the close, canonicalcentric eventization changes examined here: geometry (embedding positions) shifts, but the rulebased motif taxonomy largely persists for the most stable classes. For full numeric tables, perpermutation mappings, adjudication candidates, and the exact scripts/commit used to produce these results, consult Supplement S1 (commit 7350a47) (outputs/permutation_label_table.csv, outputs/cluster_to_motif_mapping_parseA.csv, outputs/segmentation_agreement_mapped.csv).
