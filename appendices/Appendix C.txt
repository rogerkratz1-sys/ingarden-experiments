Appendix C — MDS and Nyström technical details

Overview
Expands the mathematical and computational description of the embedding pipeline summarized in Section 2.5 and evaluated in Section 4.1. This appendix records the linearalgebra steps, eigenpair selection rules, Nyström outofsample projection, deterministic alignment, stress diagnostics, and the targeted landmarkaugmentation loop used to produce the reported embeddings. Implementation scripts and final embedding artifacts are archived in the repository for reproducibility; specific pointers are integrated below where they are most relevant.

C.1 Classical MDS and double centering
Given an (n\times n) dissimilarity matrix (D) with entries (D_{ij}), form the squared matrix (D^{(2)}) and doublecenter it to obtain the Gram matrix: [ B = -\tfrac{1}{2},J,D^{(2)},J,\qquad J = I - \tfrac{1}{n}\mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}\top. ] Eigenpairs ((\lambda_k, v_k)) of (B) with (\lambda_k>0) yield classical MDS coordinates via (X = V\Lambda{1/2}). Numerical safeguards (symmetric enforcement, smallvalue truncation) are applied in scripts/embedding_pipeline.py to ensure numerical stability and reproducible outputs.

C.2 Eigenpair selection and dimensionality choice
Eigenpairs are retained using three joint criteria: (1) positive eigenvalues only; (2) cumulative variance threshold (default ?90% of (\mathrm{trace}(B))); and (3) meaningful stress reduction when increasing dimension. These criteria justify the 3dimensional visualization used in Section 4.1 while higher dimensions (e.g., 8D) are retained for fidelity checks and rankpreservation diagnostics. The selection logic and thresholds are implemented in the pipeline and recorded in the embedding provenance.

C.3 Nyström outofsample projection (practical formula and notes)
For a nonlandmark item (x) with squared dissimilarities (D_{xL}{(2)}) to the (m) landmarks, compute the centered vector (\tilde{D}_{xL}{(2)}) consistent with doublecentering and project into the landmark eigenspace: [ x^* = -\tfrac{1}{2},\Lambda_d^{-1/2},V_d^\top,\tilde{D}_{xL}^{(2)}, ] where (\Lambda_d) and (V_d) are the retained (d)-dimensional landmark eigenpairs. In practice we:
* precompute centering terms from the landmark block for batch projection;
* apply Tikhonov regularization to (\Lambda_d^{-1/2}) when small eigenvalues occur (default (\epsilon=10^{-8}));
* verify numerical consistency by reprojecting landmarks and logging maximum coordinate deviations.
The deterministic implementation and projection logs are in scripts/embedding_pipeline.py and outputs/embeddings/nystrom_projection_log.csv. See Supplement S1 for projection diagnostics and counterexample permutations that illustrate potential geometric artifacts.

C.4 Rotational alignment and deterministic orientation
To ensure reproducible visual alignment across runs, we apply an orthonormal Procrustes rotation that places the canonical permutation along the positive (y)axis and fixes sign indeterminacy. Rotation parameters and provenance (commit hash, run date, parameter file) are recorded with the embedding artifact so visualizations are deterministic across reproductions; the rotation step is implemented in the final refinement stage of scripts/embedding_pipeline.py.

C.5 Stress definition and fidelity diagnostics
Nonmetric SMACOF stress is reported as [ \text{Stress} = \sqrt{\frac{\sum_{i<j}\bigl(d_{ij}^{\text{MDS}}-D_{ij}\bigr)^2}{\sum_{i<j}D_{ij}^2}}, ] and we complement stress with Spearman rank correlation between original dissimilarities and embedded Euclidean distances (Section 4.1 reports Spearman (\rho) values). Shepardplot data and stress summaries used to justify the 3D visualization are archived in outputs/embeddings/shepard_plot_data.csv; these diagnostics demonstrate why the 3D view balances interpretability and fidelity for motif separation.

C.6 Targeted landmark augmentation (procedure and effect)
To reduce the heavy tail of perpoint stress produced by a fixed landmark set we run a targeted augmentation loop:
1. compute perpoint stress after an initial landmark SMACOF;
2. select the top (k) higheststress nonlandmarks (defaults (k=50), then (k=100));
3. add each selected point’s (r) nearest neighbors by original (D) (default (r=20)) to the landmark set;
4. reembed the landmark submatrix and reproject nonlandmarks;
5. run a global SMACOF refinement.
This augmentation improved global rank preservation from (\rho=0.8348) to (\rho=0.8432) in our runs; augmentation parameters and logs are in outputs/embeddings/augmentation_log.csv. The augmentation procedure and its effect on the heavytail of perpoint stress are discussed in Section 4.1 and illustrated in the Shepardplot diagnostics archived with the embedding outputs.

C.7 Numerical safeguards, implementation notes, and provenance
Practical safeguards include symmetric enforcement of (B), smallvalue eigen truncation, Tikhonov regularization for nearsingular landmark submatrices, and deterministic kmeans landmark selection. All random seeds, exact commands, and provenance headers (commit 7350a47, run date, parameter file) are embedded in output artifacts. Key files for reproduction: scripts/embedding_pipeline.py, outputs/embeddings/landmark_indices.csv, outputs/embeddings/embedding_coords.npy, and checksums.md5.
