Appendix G — Preregistered Human Evaluation Plan

Overview
Describes the full preregistered protocol used to evaluate how structural motif transformations affect perceived narrative coherence. The appendix specifies stimulus selection, outcome measures, experimental procedures, exclusion rules, statistical models, power justification, and reproducibility artifacts so readers can reproduce the human study and link behavioral results to the computational taxonomy.

Pilot materials and exemplar diagnostics supporting this appendix are archived in Supplement S2 (supplement/reader_ratings_sample_all6.csv; supplement/diagnostics_per_exemplar.csv).

Purpose and scope
* Test whether motif classes (BRE, FM/EM, APD, DCO, GD, and two exemplars from Other) systematically predict human judgments of coherence.
* Use motifdefined exemplars (selected for structural properties) rather than geometric proximity to ensure alignment between the computational taxonomy and human evaluation.

Stimulus selection and materials
* Stimuli: one representative exemplar per motif class plus two contrasting exemplars from the heterogeneous Other class (one lowincoherence, one highincoherence).
* Selection criteria: exemplars chosen by deterministic motif rules and stability scores (see Appendix D perm_index references).
* Materials archived: stimulus lists, annotated exemplars, and pilot ratings are stored in supplement/S-6/human_eval/ and supplement/S-7/stimuli/ (stimulus text, perm_index, provenance_commit).

Outcome measures
* Primary: 7point Likert coherence rating.
* Secondary: comprehension accuracy (multiplechoice questions tied to event content) and reading time (milliseconds).
* Attention checks: simple content questions embedded to detect inattentive responses.


Experimental procedure and data collection
* Design: withinsubjects presentation of stimuli in randomized order with counterbalanced assignment of exemplars to participants.
* Platform: online survey platform (platform name and exact deployment script recorded in provenance).
* Timing: selfpaced reading; comprehension questions follow each stimulus.
* Preprocessing: remove trials failing attention checks or with reading times outside preregistered bounds (see Exclusion Criteria).

Exclusion criteria and data quality rules
* Attention checks: fail ?1 ? exclude participant.
* Reading time outliers: pertrial reading time < 200 ms or > 3 median absolute deviations above participant median ? exclude trial.
* Incomplete responses: participants who do not finish the survey are excluded from primary analyses.
* Preregistered handling of missing data: listwise deletion for primary mixedeffects models; sensitivity analyses use multiple imputation (scripted and archived).

Statistical modeling and inferential plan
* Primary model: mixedeffects linear regression predicting coherence rating with fixed effect for motif_primary and covariates (score_dir, disp_kappa_scaled), random intercepts for participant and item (stimulus).
* Planned contrasts: pairwise motif contrasts with Bonferroni correction; report estimated marginal means and 95% CIs.
* Secondary models: logistic mixed models for comprehension accuracy; linear models for logtransformed reading time.
* Robustness checks: include models with motif_multilabels, stability_score as moderator, and sensitivity to exclusion rules.

Power analysis and sample size justification
* Assumptions: expected medium motif effect (Cohen’s (d\approx0.5)) for primary contrasts, withinsubjects design, ICC estimates from pilot data.
* Preregistered target: N participants sufficient to achieve ?80% power for primary motif contrasts; exact numeric target and simulation code are archived in supplement/S-6/power/ and recorded in the run manifest.

Hypotheses (preregistered)
* H1: Motif class predicts coherence ratings (omnibus effect).
* H2: Higher (\mathrm{disp}_{\kappa,\alpha}^{\mathrm{scaled}}) and lower score_dir associate with lower coherence ratings.
* H3: Motif effects remain after controlling for directional displacement and comprehension accuracy.

Adjudication, preregistration, and transparency artifacts
* Preregistration record: full protocol, analysis scripts, and exact randomization seeds are archived with timestamped provenance in supplement/S-6/preregistration/.
* Adjudication linkage: stimuli and any manual label adjustments reference perm_index and adjudication logs in Supplement S2.
* Materials for replication: survey code, analysis scripts (scripts/human_eval/), and raw deidentified data (with consent metadata) are archived in the supplement folder and accompanied by checksums and the commit hash used for the definitive run.

Practical notes for readers and reusers
* Use perm_index to map each behavioral stimulus back to the computational features in Appendix D.
* Reproduce analyses with scripts/human_eval/analyze_human_data.py --input supplement/S-6/data.csv; exact commands and commit hashes are recorded in the supplement provenance headers.
* Consult stability flags (stability_score, motif_multilabels) before interpreting singleitem effects.
