Appendix H Ś Eventization sensitivity: diagnostics, interpretation, and reproducible artifacts

Overview
This appendix documents the protocol and diagnostics used to evaluate whether motif assignments discovered on the canonical Ten Virgins eventization remain meaningful under alternative eventizations. Rather than reporting agreement numbers that are mathematically forced by onetoone index alignments, we present the mapping provenance and mapping quality diagnostics that determine whether high agreement is substantive. All raw artifacts, mapping CSVs, remapped JSONs, and full numeric tables (including bootstrap outputs) are archived in Supplement S7 (paths listed below).

Evaluation protocol (concise)
* Discovery: motif exemplars and thresholds were defined on the canonical eventization only.
* Evaluation: for each alternative parse we (1) map parse units to canonical indices using the documented CSV mapping, (2) run the identical motifassignment pipeline without retuning thresholds, and (3) compute diagnostics (mapping quality, mapped coverage, threshold sweep, and motif preservation).
* Reporting principle: when remapped arrays are identical to canonical indices (mappingdriven), ARI/? are omitted from the main appendix table and replaced by mapping diagnostics; when mappings are substantive (high token overlap and broad coverage), ARI/? with bootstrap CIs are reported.

Flip diagnostics and motif preservation (summary)
* Flip attribution rules (anchor fragmentation, block fragmentation, singleelement relocation, displacement scaling, other/mixed) and perflip records are provided in Supplement S7.
* Permotif preservation (qualitative): APD and BRE are the most robust motifs across plausible eventizations; FM/EM is most sensitive to splitting/merging. Exact preservation fractions and 95% bootstrap CIs are in Supplement S7.



Why degenerate ARI/? values are omitted here (brief justification)
* When a mapping assigns parse units to canonical indices in a onetoone sequence, remapped parse labels equal canonical indices and ARI/? computed on those remapped arrays are mathematically forced to 1.000. Reporting such degenerate numbers without mapping diagnostics is uninformative. Appendix H therefore omits degenerate ARI/? from the main table and instead provides the mapping quality evidence that determines whether perfect agreement is substantive (high token overlap and nearcomplete coverage) or mappingdriven.
